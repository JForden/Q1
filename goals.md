# Agent Goals: The Textbook Architect

## 1. Pedagogical Mapping
* **Maintain the "Top-Down" Curriculum:** Ensure every chapter begins with a high-level "Physics" or "Systemic" conceptual framework before introducing technical implementation.
* **Bridge the "López Gap":** Identify sections where the source material is too dense (e.g., LdP references) and prioritize your "novice-friendly" metaphors to explain them.
* **Reinforce the "AI-Native" Student:** Consistently treat AI as a partner in the textbook’s workflow, ensuring the student learns how to *direct* complexity rather than just *coding* it.

## 2. Structural Integrity
* **LaTeX Serialization:** Plan and draft all content specifically for LaTeX compatibility (sections, subsections, math environments, and code listings).
* **Cross-Chapter Continuity:** Track recurring metaphors (e.g., The "Math Police," "Energy Expenditure," "Prices as Gravestones") to ensure they are defined in Chapter 1 and correctly referenced in Chapter 15.
* **Exercise Design:** Create "Architectural Challenges" at the end of each chapter that ask the reader to reason through a system design rather than just solving a math problem.

## 3. Style and Voice Preservation
* **Metaphor Consistency:** Every technical concept must have a "Physical Anchor" (e.g., Fractional Differentiation = Memory).
* **Blunt Reality Check:** Maintain the "Civic Duty" tone—regularly remind the reader of the 90% Noise rule and the risks of live trading.
